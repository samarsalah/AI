# Code of Conduct

**Effective Date:** January 1, 2026  
**Policy Owner:** Chief People Officer & General Counsel  
**Applies To:** All employees, officers, directors, contractors, interns, and temporary workers

---

## 1. Purpose and Commitment

This Code of Conduct defines the ethical and behavioral standards expected of everyone representing the company. We are committed to a workplace built on integrity, respect, inclusion, and accountability. Each person is responsible for reading, understanding, and following this Code. Violations may result in disciplinary action up to and including termination of employment or contract.

No policy can address every situation. When in doubt, ask: *Would I be comfortable if this decision were public?* Seek guidance from your manager, HR, or Legal.

---

## 2. Our Core Values in Practice

- **Integrity:** We are honest, transparent, and honor our commitments.
- **Respect:** We treat colleagues, customers, partners, and communities with dignity.
- **Excellence:** We deliver quality work and accept responsibility for outcomes.
- **Inclusion:** We welcome diverse perspectives and foster belonging.
- **Accountability:** We own our actions and report concerns without fear of retaliation.

---

## 3. Workplace Respect and Anti-Harassment

### 3.1 Policy Statement

The company prohibits harassment, discrimination, bullying, and retaliation based on any protected characteristic including race, color, religion, sex (including pregnancy, sexual orientation, and gender identity), national origin, age (40+), disability, genetic information, veteran status, or any other status protected by law.

Harassment includes unwelcome conduct that creates an intimidating, hostile, or offensive work environment or that interferes with work performance. This applies to all work-related settings: offices, remote meetings, off-sites, conferences, and company-sponsored social events.

### 3.2 Examples of Prohibited Conduct

- Offensive jokes, slurs, epithets, or stereotypes
- Unwelcome sexual advances, requests for sexual favors, or sexually explicit materials
- Physical intimidation, threats, or unwelcome touching
- Display of offensive images or symbols (including on virtual backgrounds)
- Deliberate misgendering or deadnaming after correction
- Exclusion or sabotage based on protected characteristics
- Cyberbullying, harassment via Slack, email, or social media directed at colleagues

### 3.3 Romantic and Personal Relationships

- Managers may not date or engage in romantic relationships with direct or indirect reports
- Relationships between peers that create real or perceived conflicts must be disclosed to HR
- HR may require role changes or reporting-line adjustments to mitigate conflicts

### 3.4 Reporting Harassment or Discrimination

**Step-by-step reporting procedure:**

1. **Document** incidents: dates, times, locations, witnesses, and descriptions of behavior.
2. **Report** through any of the following channels:
   - Direct manager (if appropriate; not required if manager is involved)
   - HR Business Partner: **hr-conduct@company.com**
   - Anonymous Ethics Hotline: **1-800-555-0199** or **ethics.company.com**
   - Chief People Officer or General Counsel directly
3. **Acknowledgment:** HR confirms receipt within **2 business days**.
4. **Assessment:** HR and Legal determine whether immediate interim measures are needed (e.g., separation of parties, paid administrative leave).
5. **Investigation:** Impartial investigation conducted within **15 business days** for standard cases; complex cases may extend with notice to complainant.
6. **Resolution:** Findings communicated to relevant parties; corrective action taken if policy violation confirmed.
7. **Follow-up:** HR checks in with complainant within **30 days** of resolution.

### 3.5 Anti-Retaliation

Retaliation against anyone who reports concerns in good faith, participates in an investigation, or refuses to engage in wrongful conduct is strictly prohibited. Retaliation is a standalone violation subject to discipline, including termination.

## 4. Conflicts of Interest

### 4.1 Definition

A conflict of interest exists when personal interests could improperly influence—or appear to influence—business judgment.

### 4.2 Common Conflict Scenarios

- Financial interest in a competitor, vendor, or customer
- Outside employment that competes or impairs performance
- Hiring or supervising family members or close friends without disclosure
- Accepting gifts, entertainment, or favors that could influence decisions
- Using company resources, time, or information for personal ventures
- Board service or advisory roles without approval

### 4.3 Disclosure and Approval Process

1. Complete **Conflict of Interest Disclosure Form (COI-01)** upon hire and **annually** each January.
2. Submit updates within **10 business days** when new potential conflicts arise.
3. HR and Legal review disclosures within **10 business days**.
4. Outcomes may include: no action, recusal from decisions, divestment, role change, or prohibition of activity.
5. Undisclosed conflicts may result in discipline.

### 4.4 Gifts and Entertainment

- Employees may not accept gifts over **$100** aggregate per vendor per year
- Cash and cash equivalents are never acceptable
- Gifts to government officials require Legal pre-approval
- See Expense Reimbursement Policy for entertainment rules

## 5. Confidentiality and Insider Information

Employees must protect confidential company and customer information during and after employment. Trading company securities while in possession of material non-public information is prohibited. Insider trading policy applies to all employees; blackout periods apply to designated roles.

---

## 6. Use of Company Resources

Company equipment, systems, and time are for business purposes. Limited personal use is permitted if it does not interfere with work, violate policy, or incur unauthorized cost. No expectation of privacy exists on company systems; monitoring may occur per law and policy.

Prohibited uses include:

- Accessing, storing, or distributing illegal content
- Installing unauthorized software
- Cryptocurrency mining on company devices
- Political campaigning using company resources

---

## 7. Health, Safety, and Substance Abuse

Employees must comply with workplace safety rules, report hazards promptly, and never work impaired by alcohol, illegal drugs, or misuse of prescription medication. The company maintains a **Drug-Free Workplace** policy. Reasonable accommodations for disabilities, including substance use disorders, are handled through HR.

Violence or threats of violence result in immediate termination and law enforcement referral.

---

## 8. External Communications and Social Media

- Only authorized spokespersons may speak to media on behalf of the company
- Social media posts must not disclose confidential information or harass colleagues
- When identifying as an employee, include disclaimer that views are personal unless posting approved company content
- Respond to negative commentary professionally; escalate threats to Security

---

## 9. Protecting Company Assets and Intellectual Property

Employees must safeguard physical and intellectual property. Inventions, code, and creative works developed using company resources or within scope of employment belong to the company unless otherwise agreed in writing. Do not use unlicensed software or third-party IP without Legal review.

---

## 10. Compliance with Laws and Regulations

Employees must comply with all applicable laws including anti-bribery (FCPA), export controls, sanctions, data privacy, and labor laws. Bribery of government officials or commercial partners is prohibited. Facilitation payments are not permitted.

## 12. Investigation and Disciplinary Standards

Investigations are conducted fairly and confidentially to the extent possible. Disciplinary measures are proportional and may include coaching, warning, suspension, demotion, termination, or legal action. Serious misconduct (violence, fraud, harassment, discrimination) may result in immediate termination.
