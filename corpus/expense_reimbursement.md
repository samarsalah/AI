# Expense Reimbursement Policy

**Effective Date:** January 1, 2026  
**Policy Owner:** Finance & Operations  
**Applies To:** All employees, contractors (where contract specifies), and authorized travelers on company business

---

## 1. Purpose

This policy establishes standards for incurring, documenting, approving, and reimbursing business expenses. The company will reimburse reasonable and necessary expenses incurred in the course of authorized business activities, provided they comply with this policy and receive required approvals **before** purchase when specified below.

Employees are stewards of company funds. Extravagant, personal, or undocumented expenses will be denied and may result in disciplinary action.

---

## 2. General Principles

- **Business purpose required:** Every expense must have a documented business justification tied to a project, client, or internal initiative.
- **No personal benefit:** Expenses primarily benefiting the employee personally are not reimbursable.
- **Pre-approval when required:** Travel and expenses above thresholds in Section 4 require advance approval.
- **Timely submission:** Expense reports must be submitted within **30 calendar days** of incurring the expense.
- **Original documentation:** Itemized receipts are required except where noted. Credit card statements alone are insufficient.
- **Tax compliance:** Reimbursements are processed according to applicable tax regulations; employees are responsible for personal tax implications of non-compliant submissions.

---

## 3. Expense Submission Workflow

### 3.1 Step-by-Step Reimbursement Process

1. **Incur expense** using company corporate card (preferred) or personal funds.
2. **Obtain itemized receipt** showing vendor, date, amount, and items purchased.
3. **Log expense** in Concur Expense within 30 days.
4. **Attach receipt** and complete required fields: business purpose, cost center, project code, attendees (for meals).
5. **Submit report** for manager approval.
6. **Manager reviews** within **3 business days** for compliance and business necessity.
7. **Finance audits** reports over $500 or flagged by policy rules within **5 business days**.
8. **Reimbursement** issued on next payroll cycle (personal card) or auto-reconciled (corporate card).

### 3.2 Corporate Card vs. Personal Reimbursement

| Method | When to Use | Notes |
|--------|-------------|-------|
| Company Amex | Travel, meals, software under $500 | Default for employees Level 5+ |
| Personal reimbursement | One-off purchases, card unavailable | Submit via Concur |
| Purchase order | Vendors over $5,000 | Procurement approval required |

Lost receipts require **Finance Exception Form (FE-02)** with manager attestation; maximum **$75** per incident, twice per year.

## 4. Travel Policy and Tiers

### 4.1 Travel Authorization Tiers

| Tier | Trip Cost Estimate | Approval Required | Lead Time |
|------|-------------------|-------------------|-----------|
| Tier 1 | Up to $1,500 | Manager | 3 business days |
| Tier 2 | $1,501 – $5,000 | Director | 5 business days |
| Tier 3 | $5,001 – $15,000 | VP + Finance | 10 business days |
| Tier 4 | Over $15,000 | CFO | 15 business days |

Submit **Travel Authorization Request (TA-01)** in Concur before booking. Emergency travel may be booked with verbal manager approval; written authorization within 24 hours is mandatory.

### 4.2 Air Travel

- **Economy class** is standard for flights under **6 hours**
- **Premium economy** permitted for flights **6–10 hours**; business class requires VP approval
- **Business class** permitted for flights over **10 hours** or for employees at Director level and above on international travel
- Book at least **14 days** in advance when possible through approved travel portal (Concur Travel)
- Lowest logical fare within ±1 hour of preferred schedule must be selected
- Personal loyalty points earned on company travel belong to the employee but may not influence fare selection

### 4.3 Lodging

| Employee Level | Nightly Cap (Domestic) | Nightly Cap (International Tier 1 Cities) |
|----------------|------------------------|-------------------------------------------|
| Individual Contributor | $200 | $275 |
| Manager | $250 | $325 |
| Director+ | $300 | $400 |

Lodging must be booked through Concur. Taxes and fees are included in caps. Extended stays over **30 nights** require separate housing policy review.

### 4.4 Ground Transportation

- **Rideshare/taxi:** Reimbursable for airport transfers and business meetings when public transit is impractical
- **Rental cars:** Standard or intermediate class only; insurance declined if covered by corporate policy
- **Personal vehicle mileage:** IRS standard mileage rate (published annually in policy appendix); odometer log required for trips over 50 miles
- **Parking and tolls:** Reimbursable with receipt

Commuting from home to regular office is **not** reimbursable.

## 5. Meal Allowances

### 5.1 Daily Meal Caps (While Traveling)

| Meal | Cap (Domestic) | Cap (International) |
|------|----------------|---------------------|
| Breakfast | $20 | $30 |
| Lunch | $35 | $45 |
| Dinner | $60 | $75 |
| Daily total | $115 | $150 |

Alcohol is **not** reimbursable unless entertaining clients per Section 5.3.

### 5.2 Non-Travel Business Meals

Team meals require manager pre-approval if expected cost exceeds **$50 per person**. Document attendees and business purpose on receipt submission.

### 5.3 Client Entertainment

- Director approval required for single events over **$500**
- Maximum **$150 per person** unless VP approval obtained
- Alcohol permitted up to **30%** of total meal cost
- List all attendees (names and companies) in expense report
- Employees may not entertain government officials without Legal pre-clearance

### 5.4 Per Diem Alternative

Employees on extended field assignments (14+ consecutive days) may elect IRS per diem rates instead of actuals with Finance approval. Election must be made before trip start and cannot be changed mid-assignment.

---

## 6. Non-Travel Reimbursable Expenses

### 6.1 Professional Development

- Up to **$2,000 per calendar year** for pre-approved courses, certifications, and conferences
- Manager approval under $500; Director approval above $500
- Reimbursement contingent on passing (if applicable) and **12-month** repayment obligation if employee leaves voluntarily within 12 months of reimbursement

### 6.2 Software and Subscriptions

Business tools under **$100/month** may be expensed with manager approval. Amounts above require IT and Procurement review for security and licensing compliance.

### 6.3 Office Supplies (Remote)

Remote employees should use stipend for routine supplies. Reimbursement for items over **$75** requires itemized receipt and manager approval.

### 6.4 Mobile Phone

Employees without company-issued phones may claim up to **$50/month** mobile stipend if role requires frequent business calls. Submit via recurring allowance form in HRIS.

---

## 7. Non-Reimbursable Expenses

The following are **not** reimbursable:

- Personal entertainment, gym, spa, or minibar charges
- Traffic violations, parking tickets, and towing fees
- Childcare or pet care during travel
- Upgrades for personal comfort (seat upgrades, room upgrades) without approval
- Lost personal items
- Political contributions
- Gifts to government employees without Legal approval
- Expenses for spouses or companions unless pre-approved client event

---

## 8. Approval Authority Matrix

| Expense Type | Approver |
|--------------|----------|
| Routine expenses under $500 | Direct manager |
| Travel Tier 2 | Director |
| Travel Tier 3–4 | VP + Finance |
| Client entertainment over $500 | Director |
| Policy exceptions | Finance Controller |
| Conflicts (manager is traveler) | Skip-level manager |

Finance retains audit rights and may deny expenses that violate policy regardless of prior approval.

---

## 9. Audit, Fraud, and Consequences

Finance conducts random audits of **10%** of reports monthly. Patterns of policy violations, duplicate submissions, or falsified receipts may result in:

- Clawback of reimbursement
- Revocation of corporate card
- Disciplinary action up to termination
- Referral to law enforcement for fraud

Report suspected misuse anonymously via **ethics@company.com**.

---

## 10. International Travel Addendum

- Obtain **Travel Security Brief** from Security for destinations on the restricted or high-risk list
- Register trip in **International SOS** portal before departure
- Visa and vaccination costs reimbursable with manager approval
- Currency conversion at actual rate; use company card to minimize conversion fees
