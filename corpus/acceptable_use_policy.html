Acceptable Use Policy

Effective Date: January 1, 2026
Policy Owner: Information Technology
Applies To: All users of company technology resources

1. Purpose

This policy defines acceptable use of company computers, networks, email, cloud services, and data. Users must protect company assets and use technology lawfully, ethically, and in support of business objectives.

2. Acceptable Use

  • Business communications and collaboration
  • Approved software development and testing
  • Limited personal use that does not interfere with work or consume excessive bandwidth
  • Security-conscious handling of company and customer data

3. Prohibited Activities

  • Unauthorized access to systems, accounts, or data
  • Sharing credentials or bypassing authentication controls
  • Installing unapproved software or browser extensions
  • Cryptocurrency mining, torrenting, or illegal content
  • Harassment, discrimination, or threats via company systems
  • Exfiltration of confidential data to personal accounts or devices

4. Email and Messaging

Company email and Slack are for business purposes. Phishing reports go to security@company.com. Auto-forwarding company email to personal accounts is prohibited without IT approval.

5. Mobile and BYOD

Personal devices accessing company email must enroll in MDM. Company may remotely wipe corporate data on separation. Restricted data must not be stored on unapproved personal devices.

6. Monitoring

The company reserves the right to monitor network traffic, system logs, and email metadata for security and compliance. Users should have no expectation of privacy on company systems except where prohibited by law.

7. Cloud Services and SaaS

Employees may only use company-approved cloud applications listed in the IT Service Catalog. Storing company data in personal Dropbox, Google Drive, or similar services is prohibited. New SaaS requests require IT Security review via ticket SEC-200.

8. Remote Access

Remote access requires company VPN and MFA. Split tunneling must remain disabled on managed devices. Contractors receive time-limited accounts reviewed every 90 days.

9. Software Licensing

All software must be properly licensed. Open-source usage must comply with the Open Source Policy OSP-100. Employees may not install pirated or unlicensed tools on company equipment.

10. Incident Reporting

Report lost devices, suspected malware, or policy violations within one hour to security@company.com or ext. 5500. Do not attempt unauthorized remediation on production systems.

11. Enforcement

Violations may result in access suspension, disciplinary action up to termination, and referral to law enforcement. Report violations to it-policy@company.com.
