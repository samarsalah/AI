# Remote Work Policy

**Effective Date:** January 1, 2026  
**Policy Owner:** Operations & People  
**Applies To:** All employees with approved remote or hybrid work arrangements

---

## 1. Purpose

This policy defines eligibility, expectations, security requirements, and compensation-related provisions for employees who perform work outside company offices. Remote work is a privilege extended when role requirements, performance, and security posture support it. The company reserves the right to modify or revoke remote arrangements with reasonable notice.

---

## 2. Work Arrangement Categories

### 2.1 Fully Remote

Employee works from an approved location (typically residence) 100% of scheduled hours. Must reside in a state or country where the company is registered to employ workers.

### 2.2 Hybrid

Employee works remotely on designated days and attends an assigned office on other days. Default hybrid schedule is **Tuesday and Thursday in-office**, unless team leadership publishes an alternate pattern.

### 2.3 Temporary Remote

Short-term approval (up to 30 consecutive days) for travel, relocation transition, or personal circumstances. Requires manager approval and HR notification.

### 2.4 Not Eligible

Roles marked "Onsite Required" in the job description (e.g., lab technicians, mailroom, certain hardware roles) are not eligible for routine remote work.

---

## 3. Eligibility and Approval Process

### 3.1 Initial Approval

**Step-by-step:**

1. Employee completes **Remote Work Request Form (RW-01)** in the HRIS.
2. Manager assesses role suitability, team coverage, and performance history.
3. IT Security reviews home network and device compliance checklist.
4. HR confirms work location is in an approved jurisdiction and updates payroll/tax records.
5. Employee signs **Remote Work Agreement (RW-A)** acknowledging this policy.
6. Final approval issued within **10 business days**.

### 3.2 Ongoing Eligibility

Remote status is reviewed annually at performance review or upon role change. Grounds for revocation include:

- Repeated failure to meet core hours or availability standards
- Security incidents attributable to remote work practices
- Sustained performance below expectations
- Business need for onsite collaboration

Revocation requires **15 business days** written notice except in cases of immediate security or conduct violations.

---

## 4. Core Hours and Availability

### 4.1 Standard Core Hours

Unless otherwise approved, all remote employees must be available during **core hours: 10:00 AM – 3:00 PM** in their **assigned time zone** (default: time zone of hiring office).

### 4.2 Availability Requirements

- Respond to Slack/email within **30 minutes** during core hours
- Attend all scheduled meetings with camera on when requested by meeting owner
- Maintain calendar accuracy with "Focus Time" blocks for deep work
- Be reachable by phone during core hours

### 4.3 Flexible Schedules

Employees may request alternate schedules (e.g., early start/end) via manager approval. Requests must document how core-hour coverage will be maintained or how the team will be notified of temporary unavailability.

### 4.4 Cross-Time-Zone Collaboration

Employees working more than **3 hours** offset from their team's primary zone must attend a weekly overlap window of at least **2 hours** with the team, scheduled by the manager.

---

## 5. Work Environment Standards

### 5.1 Dedicated Workspace

Remote employees must maintain a professional, safe workspace:

- Adequate lighting and ergonomics (chair, desk height)
- Reliable internet: minimum **50 Mbps download / 10 Mbps upload**
- Private area for confidential calls when handling sensitive information
- Compliance with local health and safety regulations

### 5.2 Professional Conduct During Video Calls

- Neutral or professional background (company-branded virtual backgrounds permitted)
- Minimize background noise and interruptions
- Dress code: business casual minimum for external and leadership meetings

### 5.3 Dependent and Caregiving Distractions

Brief interruptions are understood; sustained childcare or dependent care during work hours is not a substitute for approved leave. Employees needing schedule adjustments should discuss flexible arrangements with HR.

---

## 6. Equipment and Stipend Eligibility

### 6.1 Company-Provided Equipment

Standard issue for approved remote employees:

- Laptop (company-managed)
- External monitor (one 27" display upon initial remote approval)
- Keyboard and mouse if requested
- Headset with noise cancellation

All company equipment remains company property and must be returned within **5 business days** of separation or arrangement change.

### 6.2 Remote Work Stipend

| Employee Type | Monthly Stipend | Eligibility |
|---------------|-----------------|-------------|
| Fully remote (permanent) | $75/month | Approved RW-A on file |
| Hybrid | $40/month | Approved hybrid schedule |
| Temporary remote | $0 | Use company equipment only |

**Stipend rules:**

- Paid as taxable compensation on regular payroll
- Intended for internet, utilities, and incidental home office costs
- Not available to employees who receive a dedicated coworking membership paid by the company
- Stipend begins the pay period following RW-A approval
- Stipend ends when employee returns to full-time onsite status

### 6.3 Coworking and Ergonomic Requests

One-time ergonomic assessments may be reimbursed up to **$500** with HR pre-approval. Coworking space reimbursement up to **$300/month** requires VP approval and documented inability to work effectively from home.

## 7. Information Security Protocols

Remote employees must adhere to all requirements in the **Information Security Policy** and the following remote-specific controls.

### 7.1 Network Security

- **Prohibited:** Public unsecured Wi-Fi for accessing company systems without company VPN
- **Required:** Connect via company VPN (Zscaler) when accessing internal applications, source code, or customer data
- Home routers must use WPA2 or WPA3 encryption; default admin passwords must be changed

### 7.2 Device Security

- Company laptops must have full-disk encryption enabled (managed by IT)
- Personal devices may not store company confidential data unless enrolled in MDM (Mobile Device Management)
- Screen lock after **5 minutes** of inactivity is mandatory
- Report lost or stolen devices to **security@company.com** within **1 hour**

### 7.3 Physical Security

- Lock screens when stepping away
- Store printed confidential materials in locked storage; shred when discarded
- Do not allow household members or visitors to view confidential information
- Company confidential calls must not be taken on speakerphone in public spaces

### 7.4 Software and Access

- Install software only through approved channels (Company Portal / IT ticketing)
- Admin/root privileges on company devices are disabled
- Quarterly access reviews apply to all remote workers with elevated permissions

### 7.5 Incident Reporting

Suspected phishing, malware, or data exposure must be reported immediately via the **Security Hotline** (ext. 5500) or `#security-incidents` Slack channel. Do not wait for manager approval before reporting.

---

## 8. Data Privacy and Location Disclosure

Employees must notify HR **before** relocating to a new state or country for more than **30 days**. Unauthorized relocation may result in tax, legal, and employment compliance violations and disciplinary action.

The company monitors login geography for anomaly detection. VPN use to obscure true location without approval is prohibited.

---

## 9. Performance and Communication Expectations

- Remote employees are subject to the same performance standards as onsite employees
- Managers conduct at least **monthly 1:1s** and quarterly goal reviews
- Employees must attend mandatory onsite events (e.g., annual all-hands, team offsites) with **30 days** notice; travel covered per Expense Reimbursement Policy

---

## 10. Expenses and Travel

Routine commuting to a coworking space is not reimbursable. Business travel from the remote work location to offices or clients follows standard travel approval tiers. See **expense_reimbursement.md** for details.

---

## 11. Policy Violations

Violations may result in:

- Written warning and mandatory security retraining
- Suspension of remote privileges
- Termination for serious or repeated breaches

---

## 12. Questions and Updates

Contact **remote-work@company.com** for arrangement changes. This policy is reviewed annually.
