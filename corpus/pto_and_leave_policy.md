# Paid Time Off and Leave Policy

**Effective Date:** January 1, 2026  
**Policy Owner:** Human Resources  
**Applies To:** All full-time and part-time employees (excludes contractors unless stated in contract)

---

## 1. Purpose and Scope

This policy establishes the rules, accrual methods, approval procedures, and employee obligations related to paid time off (PTO), sick leave, parental leave, and other leave categories. The company recognizes that rest, recovery, and family responsibilities are essential to sustainable performance. This document is the authoritative reference for managers and employees when planning time away from work.

Employees are expected to coordinate leave with their manager and team to minimize disruption. Abuse of leave provisions, falsification of records, or failure to follow notification procedures may result in disciplinary action up to and including termination.

---

## 2. Definitions

- **PTO (Paid Time Off):** A combined bank of vacation and personal days used for planned absences, rest, travel, appointments, and discretionary time away.
- **Sick Leave:** Time used specifically for illness, injury, medical appointments, or caring for an immediate family member with a qualifying health need.
- **Accrual Year:** The 12-month period from each employee's hire anniversary date (or January 1 for employees hired before 2024, per legacy grandfathering).
- **Eligible Employee:** An employee who has completed their probationary period (90 days) and maintains active employment status.
- **Parental Leave:** Job-protected leave following birth, adoption, or foster placement of a child.

---

## 3. Vacation and PTO Accrual

### 3.1 Accrual Rates (Full-Time Employees)

PTO accrues per pay period based on tenure:

| Tenure | Annual PTO Days | Accrual Per Pay Period (Biweekly) |
|--------|-----------------|-----------------------------------|
| 0–2 years | 15 days (120 hours) | 4.62 hours |
| 3–5 years | 20 days (160 hours) | 6.15 hours |
| 6–10 years | 25 days (200 hours) | 7.69 hours |
| 11+ years | 30 days (240 hours) | 9.23 hours |

Part-time employees (20–29 hours/week) accrue PTO on a pro-rata basis calculated from their scheduled weekly hours divided by 40.

### 3.2 Accrual Rules

- PTO begins accruing on the first day of employment but cannot be used until the probationary period ends.
- Accrual occurs only during active pay status. Unpaid leave suspends accrual.
- Maximum PTO carryover at year-end is **40 hours** (5 days). Excess balance is forfeited unless a written exception is approved by HR and the employee's VP.
- PTO does not accrue during suspension without pay.
- Upon termination, unused accrued PTO is paid out in the final paycheck in accordance with state law.

### 3.3 Requesting PTO

**Step-by-step procedure:**

1. Review team calendar and project deadlines.
2. Submit a PTO request in the HRIS (Workday) at least **two weeks** in advance for absences of 3+ consecutive days, or **48 hours** in advance for single-day requests.
3. Manager reviews and approves or denies within **2 business days**.
4. If denied, manager must provide a business reason and propose alternate dates when possible.
5. Employee sets an out-of-office message and delegates critical tasks before departure.
6. Employee records actual time taken in the timekeeping system on return if not auto-synced.

Managers may not deny PTO solely to avoid inconvenience; denials must be based on documented business-critical needs (e.g., go-live windows, audit periods, or insufficient coverage).

## 4. Sick Leave

### 4.1 Allocation

All eligible employees receive **10 sick days (80 hours)** per accrual year, front-loaded on January 1 (or hire date anniversary for employees in states requiring accrual-based sick leave). Sick leave does not carry over except where required by local law.

### 4.2 Permitted Uses

Sick leave may be used for:

- Personal illness or injury
- Medical, dental, or mental health appointments
- Recovery from medical procedures
- Care for a spouse, domestic partner, child, parent, or household member with a qualifying health condition
- Public health emergencies when the employee or a dependent is subject to quarantine or isolation orders

### 4.3 Notification Requirements

- **Same-day illness:** Notify manager before scheduled start time or within 1 hour of shift start. Use the designated sick-reporting channel (Slack `#sick-report` or HRIS absence type "Sick").
- **Extended illness (3+ consecutive days):** Provide manager and HR with expected return date. For absences exceeding **5 consecutive workdays**, a fitness-for-duty note from a licensed provider may be required before return.
- Employees must not perform work while on sick leave unless transitioning to a formal remote-work accommodation approved by HR.

### 4.4 Sick Leave vs. PTO

Employees should use sick leave for health-related absences. Using PTO for illness is permitted when sick leave is exhausted. Managers may not require disclosure of diagnosis; only confirmation that the absence is health-related is needed.

## 5. Parental Leave

### 5.1 Eligibility

Employees who have completed **6 months** of continuous service are eligible for parental leave. Both birthing and non-birthing parents, as well as adoptive and foster parents, are eligible.

### 5.2 Leave Duration and Pay

| Leave Type | Duration | Pay |
|------------|----------|-----|
| Primary caregiver | 12 weeks | 100% base salary for 8 weeks; 60% for weeks 9–12 |
| Secondary caregiver | 6 weeks | 100% base salary for 4 weeks; 60% for weeks 5–6 |
| Adoption / foster placement | 8 weeks | 100% base salary for 6 weeks; 60% for weeks 7–8 |

Pay is calculated on base salary only; variable compensation (bonus, commission) is excluded unless required by local statute.

### 5.3 Parental Leave Procedure

1. **Notice:** Employee notifies manager and HR at least **30 days** before anticipated leave start when foreseeable; as soon as practicable otherwise.
2. **Documentation:** Submit HR Form PL-100 (Parental Leave Request) with expected due date, adoption match date, or placement date.
3. **Planning meeting:** HR schedules a leave planning call within 5 business days to review pay, benefits continuation, return date, and coverage plan.
4. **Benefits:** Health benefits continue during paid leave. During unpaid portions (if any), employee pays employee share via COBRA or payroll deduction as applicable.
5. **Return:** Employee receives a return-to-work confirmation from HR at least 1 week before scheduled return. Flexible return (part-time ramp) may be requested for up to 4 weeks and requires manager approval.
6. **Job protection:** Employee returns to the same or equivalent role with equivalent pay and benefits, subject to legitimate business restructuring that would have affected the role regardless of leave.

### 5.4 Interaction with FMLA and State Leave

Where federal FMLA or state family leave laws provide greater rights, those laws apply. HR will coordinate concurrent leave designations and provide required notices within 5 business days of leave request.

## 6. Other Leave Categories

### 6.1 Bereavement Leave

- **Immediate family** (spouse, partner, child, parent, sibling): 5 paid days
- **Extended family** (grandparent, in-law, aunt/uncle): 3 paid days
- Additional unpaid leave may be requested through HR.

### 6.2 Jury Duty and Civic Obligations

Paid leave for jury duty or witness service up to **10 days** per calendar year. Employee must provide summons documentation within 5 days of receipt.

### 6.3 Military Leave

Unpaid leave per USERRA. Differential pay may apply for reservists per separate Military Leave Addendum.

### 6.4 Unpaid Personal Leave

Unpaid leave of 1–12 weeks may be approved for educational pursuits, personal matters, or after PTO exhaustion. Requires director-level approval and HR documentation. Benefits may be employee-paid during unpaid periods.

---

## 7. Holidays and Company Closures

The company observes 10 paid holidays annually (list published each November). If a holiday falls during PTO, that day is not charged to PTO. Mandatory company closures (e.g., year-end shutdown) may require use of PTO unless otherwise announced.

---

## 8. Manager Responsibilities

- Approve or deny leave requests promptly and consistently.
- Ensure team coverage without discouraging lawful use of leave.
- Never retaliate against employees for using sick or parental leave.
- Report patterns of absenteeism to HR when they may indicate accommodation needs or performance issues.
- Maintain confidentiality of health-related leave information.

---

## 9. Compliance and Questions

This policy is governed by applicable federal, state, and local laws. Where conflict exists, the more employee-favorable provision applies unless prohibited by law. Questions should be directed to **hr-leave@company.com** or the HR Business Partner assigned to your department.
