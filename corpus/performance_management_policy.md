# Performance Management Policy

**Effective Date:** January 1, 2026  
**Policy Owner:** Human Resources  
**Applies To:** All employees and people managers

---

## 1. Purpose

This policy establishes a consistent framework for setting goals, providing feedback, evaluating performance, and supporting employee development. Effective performance management aligns individual contributions with company objectives and fosters a culture of accountability and growth.

---

## 2. Performance Cycle

The annual performance cycle runs **January 1 – December 31** with the following milestones:

| Phase | Timing | Activities |
|-------|--------|------------|
| Goal setting | January | OKRs and role goals documented in HRIS |
| Mid-year check-in | June–July | Manager-employee 1:1 review of progress |
| Year-end review | November–December | Self-assessment, manager rating, calibration |
| Merit & promotion | January–April | Compensation and promotion decisions |

---

## 3. Goal Setting

### 3.1 Requirements

Each employee must have **3–5 measurable goals** by January 31, approved by their manager. Goals should follow SMART criteria (Specific, Measurable, Achievable, Relevant, Time-bound) and include at least one development goal.

### 3.2 OKR Alignment

Department OKRs cascade from company objectives published in Q1. Individual goals must demonstrably support team OKRs. Managers document alignment in the goal-setting module.

### 3.3 Goal Changes

Material goal changes after Q1 require manager approval and HR notification if they affect bonus eligibility or role scope.

---

## 4. Ongoing Feedback

- **Weekly 1:1s:** Managers hold regular 1:1 meetings (minimum biweekly for teams under 8)
- **Real-time feedback:** Encouraged via Slack, email, or in-person; document significant feedback in HRIS when it affects performance trajectory
- **360 feedback:** Optional for senior roles; mandatory for Manager+ promotions
- **Skip-level meetings:** Quarterly for ICs; employees may request agenda items via HR

---

## 5. Performance Ratings

### 5.1 Rating Scale

| Rating | Definition | Typical Distribution Guidance |
|--------|------------|-------------------------------|
| 5 – Exceptional | Consistently exceeds all expectations; enterprise impact | ≤10% |
| 4 – Exceeds | Frequently exceeds expectations | ~20% |
| 3 – Meets | Reliably achieves expectations | ~60% |
| 2 – Developing | Partially meets; improvement plan needed | ~10% |
| 1 – Unsatisfactory | Does not meet expectations | Rare |

Distributions are guidelines; calibration sessions ensure fairness across teams.

### 5.2 Self-Assessment

Employees complete self-assessments in HRIS by **November 15**. Self-assessments should cite accomplishments, challenges, and development areas with specific examples.

### 5.3 Manager Evaluation

Managers submit ratings and narrative feedback by **December 1**. Feedback must be behavioral, specific, and forward-looking. Ratings require skip-level review before release.

---

## 6. Performance Improvement Plans (PIPs)

When performance is rated 1 or 2, or when conduct issues affect work output, a PIP may be initiated.

**PIP procedure:**

1. HR partners with manager to draft PIP with clear objectives and timeline (typically **30–60 days**)
2. Employee receives written PIP in meeting with manager and HR present
3. Weekly check-ins document progress
4. Outcomes: return to good standing, extension, role change, or separation

PIPs are confidential and stored in HRIS restricted folders.

---

## 7. Promotions

Promotions require:

- Sustained performance at next-level expectations (typically 6+ months)
- Business need and budget approval
- Updated job description and compensation review
- VP approval for Director+ promotions

Employees may express interest via career conversations; managers initiate promotion nominations during calibration.

---

## 8. Low Performer and Separation Process

Involuntary separations for performance follow PIP completion or documented pattern of failure to meet standards. HR must approve all involuntary terminations. Severance, if offered, follows Separation Guidelines SG-200.

---

## 9. Manager Responsibilities

Managers must:

- Set clear expectations and document goals
- Provide timely, honest feedback
- Avoid bias in ratings; complete unconscious bias training annually
- Never retaliate against employees for good-faith feedback or participation in reviews
