# Data Privacy Policy

**Effective Date:** January 1, 2026  
**Policy Owner:** Privacy Office & Legal  
**Applies To:** All employees, contractors, and processors handling personal data

---

## 1. Purpose

This policy defines how the company collects, uses, stores, shares, and protects personal data belonging to employees, customers, partners, and website visitors. It supports compliance with GDPR, CCPA/CPRA, and other applicable privacy regulations.

---

## 2. Scope and Definitions

- **Personal Data:** Information relating to an identified or identifiable individual
- **Sensitive Personal Data:** Health, biometric, racial/ethnic origin, union membership, precise geolocation (where regulated)
- **Processing:** Any operation on personal data (collection, storage, use, disclosure, deletion)
- **Data Subject:** The individual whose personal data is processed
- **Controller / Processor:** Roles defined under GDPR; company is typically Controller for employee and customer data

---

## 3. Privacy Principles

1. **Lawfulness, fairness, transparency** — process with legal basis and clear notices
2. **Purpose limitation** — collect for specified, legitimate purposes
3. **Data minimization** — adequate, relevant, limited to necessity
4. **Accuracy** — keep data accurate and up to date
5. **Storage limitation** — retain only as long as needed per retention schedule
6. **Integrity and confidentiality** — appropriate security per Information Security Policy
7. **Accountability** — document processing and demonstrate compliance

---

## 4. Lawful Bases for Processing

| Context | Typical Legal Basis |
|---------|---------------------|
| Employment | Contract, legal obligation, legitimate interest |
| Payroll & benefits | Legal obligation, contract |
| Marketing (opt-in regions) | Consent |
| Security monitoring | Legitimate interest, legal obligation |
| Product analytics (aggregated) | Legitimate interest with opt-out where required |

Legal must approve new processing activities via Privacy Impact Assessment (PIA).

---

## 5. Employee Data

### 5.1 Collection

HR collects data necessary for employment: identity, contact, tax, benefits, performance, and payroll. Background checks require consent where required by law.

### 5.2 Access and Correction

Employees may access their HR data via Workday self-service. Correction requests go to **hr-privacy@company.com**; HR responds within **30 days**.

### 5.3 Monitoring

Workplace systems may be monitored per Information Security Policy. Expectation of privacy is limited on company devices and networks.

---

## 6. Customer and User Data

### 6.1 Privacy Notices

Customer-facing products must display privacy notices describing categories collected, purposes, retention, rights, and contact information. Legal approves all notices before publication.

### 6.2 Consent Management

Marketing emails require opt-in where mandated. Unsubscribe links must work within **10 days**. Cookie banners implemented for EEA/UK visitors per Cookie Policy CP-100.

### 6.3 Data Subject Requests (DSRs)

DSR types: access, deletion, correction, portability, restriction, objection.

**DSR procedure:**

1. Request received via **privacy@company.com** or web form
2. Identity verification within **5 business days**
3. Response within **30 days** (45 for complex cases with notice)
4. Log request in Privacy Request Register
5. Escalate denials to Privacy Office and Legal

---

## 7. Data Sharing and Transfers

- Third-party processors require Data Processing Agreements (DPAs)
- International transfers use Standard Contractual Clauses or adequacy decisions
- Sub-processor changes notified per DPA terms
- No sale of personal data as defined under CCPA

---

## 8. Breach Notification

Suspected personal data breaches must be reported to **privacy@company.com** and Security within **1 hour**. Privacy Office assesses notification obligations to regulators (72 hours under GDPR where applicable) and affected individuals.

---

## 9. Retention and Deletion

Follow Records Retention Schedule RS-500. Deletion requests honored unless legal hold or exception applies. Secure deletion methods per Information Security Policy.

---

## 10. Training and Accountability

- Annual privacy training mandatory for all employees
- Role-based training for Engineering, Sales, Marketing, and HR
- Privacy champions designated per department

Violations may result in disciplinary action up to termination and regulatory penalties.
